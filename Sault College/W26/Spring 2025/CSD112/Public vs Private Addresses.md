# Public IP Address
- A **public IP** is used on the **internet**.
- It’s **unique** worldwide — no two devices share the same public IP.
- Assigned by your **Internet Service Provider (ISP)**.
- It allows devices to **communicate over the internet**.

**Example:** `8.8.8.8` (Google’s DNS server)
**Used by:**
- Websites, servers, and routers that connect directly to the internet.
---
# Private IP Address
- A **private IP** is used **inside local networks** (like your home Wi-Fi).
- It’s **not visible on the internet** — it stays within your local network.
- Multiple networks can reuse the same private IP ranges safely.
- Your router uses **NAT (Network Address Translation)** to connect these devices to the internet using a single public IP.

**Common private IP ranges:**
- `10.0.0.0 – 10.255.255.255`
- `172.16.0.0 – 172.31.255.255`
- `192.168.0.0 – 192.168.255.255`

**Used by:**
- Computers, phones, printers, and smart devices on a home or office network.
