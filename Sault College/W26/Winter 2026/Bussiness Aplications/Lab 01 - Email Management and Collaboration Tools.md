# Email Organization and Management

**What is [[Email Organization]]?**

Email organization refers to the systematic approach of managing incoming and outgoing messages to maintain efficiency and reduce clutter. In professional settings, you may receive hundreds of emails daily, making organization critical.

**Why it matters:**

- Prevents important messages from being lost in cluttered inboxes
- Saves time by automating repetitive sorting tasks
- Creates a logical structure for finding information later
- Reduces cognitive load and stress from email overload

**Key tool: [[Message Rules]]**

Message rules (also called **email filters** or **inbox rules**) are automated instructions that tell your email client how to handle incoming messages based on specific criteria.

> [!NOTE] Common use cases
>- Moving emails from specific courses into dedicated folders
> - Automatically marking newsletters as read
> - Flagging urgent messages from your supervisor
> - Deleting spam or promotional emails
> 

# Calendar Management and Meeting Scheduling

Digital calendaring involves using software to organize time, schedule events, coordinate with others, and manage commitments. 

**Key concepts:**

**[[Meeting Invites]]** are digital requests sent through calendar applications

**[[Calendar Integration]]** refers to how calendar systems connect with email and other tools. When you send a meeting invite through [[Microsoft Outlook]]

**Meeting vs. Appointment:**

- **Meeting** – Involves other people; sends invites
- **Appointment** – Personal reminder; no attendees


# Email Recall Functionality

**What is [[Email Recall]]?**

Email recall is a feature that attempts to retrieve or replace an email you've already sent. 

**Why it matters:**

- Allows correction of mistakes (wrong recipient, typos, incorrect attachments)
- Can prevent embarrassing or problematic communication
- Provides a safety net for hasty sends

# Email Categorization

**What are [[Email Categories]]?**

Categories are visual labels (usually color-coded) that you apply to emails to group them by project, priority, topic, or any custom classification system you create.

> [!NOTE]
> Categories complement folders: folders physically organize emails, while categories add metadata for flexible viewing.
>
